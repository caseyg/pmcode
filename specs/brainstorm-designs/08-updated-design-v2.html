Step 3 of 7
Share Your Product Context
As a PM, your most valuable contribution to the repo is product context — the goals, constraints, and decisions that help the team (and the AI) make better choices. Let's add yours.
What product context looks like
PRDs & specs — drop them in a /docs folder
Planning notes — priorities, tradeoffs, open questions
Team instructions — how the AI should approach this project
When these live in your project, every team member's AI reads them automatically.
Try these:
"Create a product context doc for our Q2 goals"

💡 Why this matters
When engineers use AI coding agents, those agents read the same project files. Your product context shapes every AI-generated PR, spec, and decision. You're not just documenting — you're steering the AI.

Guide: Sharing Product Context with Your Team
1. Your project is a shared workspace — what a repo is, in PM terms
2. How saving & sharing works — commit = save, push = share, pull = get updates
3. Share your product context — PRDs, planning docs, instructions files
4. See what the team's been up to — "summarize recent changes", review history
5. Work on your own branch — safe space to make changes without breaking anything
6. Propose changes for review — PRs as "hey team, what do you think?"
7. Undo anything, anytime — "sort out this mess", recover lost work

5. Score and prioritize — impact vs. effort, roadmap alignment
